The primary objective of this project is to process, end-to-end, a raw and unstructured dataset consisting of more than 0.5 million (500,000+) records belonging to an e-commerce company and transform it into meaningful business intelligence insights. In today's data-driven business environment, an organization's ability to gain a competitive advantage depends on converting raw data into evidence-based decisions rather than relying on predictive assumptions. Accordingly, this project aims to eliminate operational inefficiencies and support strategic decision-making processes through data-driven analysis. Within this scope, Microsoft SQL Server was selected as the Relational Database Management System (RDBMS) for data optimization, storage, and querying processes. The raw and unstructured dataset was processed through an ETL pipeline using Python libraries, cleansed by removing missing values and outliers, and transformed into a consistent and reliable structure. Subsequently, the cleaned dataset was migrated to the Microsoft SQL Server architecture to ensure secure, high-performance, and relational storage of large-scale data, while advanced data analytics were conducted through T-SQL queries developed on SQL Server. The implemented system architecture provides analytical solutions to critical business challenges encountered in e-commerce management. For marketing and advertising optimization, DATEPART(HOUR, ...) and time-based analytical functions were utilized to identify the peak periods of customer traffic and sales conversions, enabling more effective allocation of advertising budgets and improved cost efficiency. In logistics and supply chain management, analyses of the Top 10 best-selling products and category-based dynamic inventory assessments were performed to optimize warehouse organization and accelerate logistics operations. Furthermore, within the scope of Customer Relationship Management (CRM) and loyalty programs, CustomerID-based spending behavior and purchase frequency analyses were conducted to identify customer segments with the highest Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), providing a data-driven foundation for personalized marketing campaigns and coupon strategies. As a result, this project represents a data engineering and business intelligence solution that processes raw data through the integration of Python and Microsoft SQL Server, contributing to increased revenue, reduced operational costs, and enhanced operational excellence for the enterprise.
